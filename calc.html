Does this price seem too high?

Keep in mind that this calculator only serves as an estimate for pricing your crafts. If the price seems too high, consider a few things:

  • What are the biggest factors contributing to the cost? (Usually it's cost of materials or amount of time.)
  • Can you find more affordable raw materials elsewhere to lower production costs?
  • Can you create a more efficient manufacturing technique to reduce production time?
  • Increase the perceived value of your products. Consider rebranding, perhaps updating your website, logo, and packaging in order to attract a clientele who understands that the pricing of your products accurately reflects the quality.
  • Have a few less expensive items available in addition to the higher-priced items. These do well as quick-sell items and provide products for all types of customers.

Does this price seem too low?

If you are selling products, but just don't seem to be making a profit, or even breaking even, perhaps your items are priced too low.

Pricing your items on the lower end may draw in more customers, however, you are not valuing yourself as a crafter and are not implementing good business practices. Consider these points:

  • Crafting can be very costly. You want to be sure that you are charging enough to cover your cost of materials, as well as packaging, tools, electricity, gas and any other hidden costs that go into producing this product.
  • Don't sell yourself short! Crafting can be extremely time-consuming, and is often a labor of love. If you don't set your prices to accurately reflect that you value your work, then no one else will value your work either! Pay yourself a healthy, fair hourly wage.
  • Can you possibly invest in better quality raw materials, or packaging? Not only will your finished product look and feel nicer, you can accurately reflect these improvements in your pricing.

Finally, remember the law of exchange!

Creating something with your hands, your time, and your resources requires an investment of your energy. When you provide a product or service for someone, you are loving them, helping them and assisting them. Energy has a flow, or currency. Currency is also the term for money. When you expend your energy making a product you are generating currency. It is only natural for your consumers to then give you another type of currency as payment: their money. It is a simple act of transference and exchange.
So remember, value and take pride in your work, and the benefits will flow to you!
